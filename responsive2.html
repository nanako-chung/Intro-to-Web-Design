Across the Brooklyn Bridge lies a slower, more laid back borough with a diverse landscape: Brooklyn. As New York City's second biggest borough, every neighborhood you visit is drastically different from one another. From Fort Greene Park in Downtown Brooklyn to Kings Highway in Uptown Brooklyn to the famous Coney Island Beach, Brooklyn has so much to offer if you're looking to spend a great day away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Here are some of my favorite places to visit in this borough!

Just off the coast of Coney Island is a beach called Brighton that hardly any tourists visit. It is rather isolated, but very safe and clean area that defies all expectations of a New York City beach. Reviews of the beach and tips for visiting can be found here

.

My favorite and most memorable park in Brooklyn is Fort Greene. Right by Brooklyn Tech, the most diverse and largest high school in America, Fort Greene gives a great persective on what it is like to be a high school student in Brooklyn.
